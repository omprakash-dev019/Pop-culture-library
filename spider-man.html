THE ORIGIN
STORY

"With great power comes great responsibility."

PETER BENJAMIN PARKER was an ordinary high school student in Forest Hills, Queens — until the fateful day a radioactive spider bit him during a school field trip to a science exhibit.

The spider's mutagenic venom transformed Peter, granting him superhuman strength, agility, and a preternatural "spider-sense" that warns of incoming danger. Being a scientific prodigy, Peter engineered his own web-shooters — mechanical devices worn on the wrists capable of firing sticky webbing strong enough to support a car.

Tragedy struck when a burglar Peter refused to stop killed his beloved Uncle Ben. That night, Peter Parker learned the lesson that would define his life as a hero forever.

Donning a hand-sewn red-and-blue costume, THE AMAZING SPIDER-MAN was born — protector of New York City and one of Earth's Mightiest Heroes.

SPIDER-MAN:
MENACE OR MARVEL?
This reporter has long maintained that the masked vigilante known as "Spider-Man" poses an unacceptable threat to New York's law and order. While the so-called hero claims to protect our streets, his reckless web-swinging has caused millions in property damage.

"He's a menace!" said J. Jonah Jameson, Daily Bugle publisher. "Hiding behind a mask, above the law, accountable to no one."

However, a recent poll suggests 78% of New Yorkers hold a favorable view of the Wall-Crawler — a number that has steadily risen since the Battle of New York. Spider-Man could not be reached for comment.
GOBLIN ATTACK
ON TIMES SQUARE
Chaos erupted in midtown Manhattan last Tuesday as the notorious Green Goblin unleashed a pumpkin bomb barrage across Times Square. Dozens were injured and property damage is estimated at $14 million.

Spider-Man arrived within minutes, neutralizing explosive devices and evacuating civilians before engaging the Goblin in aerial combat. Police confirmed zero fatalities.

"He saved my kid," said tourist Linda Walsh of Ohio. "Whatever Jameson says about him, that man is a hero."
OSCORP TOWER
INCIDENT REPORT
Oscorp Industries issued a statement denying responsibility for the structural damage sustained during last week's confrontation between Spider-Man and an unidentified multi-armed assailant believed to be Dr. Otto Octavius.

The Spider-Man-Octopus showdown leveled two floors of the research wing. Norman Osborn, Oscorp CEO, was unavailable for comment but sources indicate a $200M insurance claim has been filed.

City council is demanding answers about private lab safety regulations.
